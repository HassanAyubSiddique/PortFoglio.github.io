Warehouse Management System (VMS) Project

The project involves transitioning to a paperless inventory management system. The goal is to efficiently record stock by best before date (BBD) and implement stock rotation. This includes booking finished and bought-in products using SKU codes and BBD date codes, as well as managing raw materials and packaging within the same system. The system should identify product batches based on date codes when receiving order details and generate reports for product details, quantities, and locations.

In exploring existing solutions, it's crucial to review paperless inventory management systems within the food industry. Focus on how these systems handle stock recording, especially emphasizing best before dates and stock rotation. Investigate systems that efficiently manage SKU codes and BBD dates, facilitating the booking of products. Additionally, look for literature on systems that effectively handle raw materials and packaging within a unified application.

User management is key, and it's essential to find literature on systems that tailor access permissions based on employee roles. Dynamic creation of products, including editing and deletion, should be explored in terms of attributes such as product name, picture, and shelf life. Investigate systems incorporating data analytics for purchase orders, products, and inventory levels, paying attention to dynamic charts and customizable reports.

Explore alert systems that address inventory expiration and low stock counts relative to pending orders, understanding how timely notifications enhance decision-making. Lastly, delve into literature discussing responsive design for applications, ensuring they function seamlessly across both desktop and tablet devices. This comprehensive review will provide insights into existing solutions and best practices for the successful implementation of the proposed warehouse system for Rakusens.
